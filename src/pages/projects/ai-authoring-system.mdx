## The problem

Content authoring ran on **18 numbered prompts** (`learning-1` … `learning-18`) that conflated three different things: pipeline stages, always-on style rules, and standalone tasks. Authors manually sequenced prompts and re-attached files, and style rules were silently forgotten mid-run. Worse, the quality check let the model grade its own output—and **an LLM that grades itself is untrustworthy.** The risk: confidently-wrong technical content shipping to developers at scale.

## Architecture & why

- **Decision: map each concern to the correct primitive.** *Alternative rejected:* keeping one numbered-prompt chain. *Why:* the chain forced manual sequencing and silently dropped style rules. A coordinator agent + five per-stage skills handle the pipeline, `applyTo` instruction files handle always-on style, and named prompts handle standalone tasks—each concern in the primitive built for it.
- **Decision: split judge (`learning-critique`) and fixer (`learning-refine`) into skills that never merge.** *Alternative rejected:* one self-correcting skill. *Why:* a thing must not grade itself—separation keeps the evaluation honest.
- **Decision: a three-part hard gate—total ≥ 80 AND every category ≥ its floor AND no critical-error veto—enforced by a zero-dependency Node validator.** *Alternative rejected:* gating on the composite total alone. *Why:* a high total launders a fluent-but-wrong lesson past 80. The validator makes the decision *arithmetic, not vibes*: it schema-validates the score block, range-checks each category, verifies total equals the sum, computes pass/block, and fails loud on malformed input.
- **Decision: a single source of truth (`gate-config.json`) for threshold, floors, and veto.** *Alternative rejected:* inline constants per skill. *Why:* the judge, the loop, and the calibration all read the same values—inline constants would drift across the three consumers.

## Evals / validation

- A **calibration meta-eval** runs the judge against a frozen, human-approved **gold set of 10 items** across two personas and severity bands—including **2 adversarial fluent-but-wrong items** (planted factual and objective-misalignment errors) that test the *veto*, not just correlation.
- An independent judge run produced **0 false-passes**, with mean absolute total error ~4.4/100 (the judge runs slightly *stricter* than the humans—the safe direction).
- A companion automation-recommender skill ships an inventory of **93 existing automations** (14 prompts, 17 skills, 16 agents, 46 instructions) for overlap detection before new ones are built.

## Outcome

- **18 numbered prompts → a layered system** of agents, skills, and instruction files—each authoring concern handled by the right primitive.
- A quality gate that **can't be gamed by a high average**: 0 false-passes at threshold against a gold set that deliberately includes fluent-but-wrong traps.
- The system supports human-in-the-loop gating today; the architecture is the durable contribution—evals and a deterministic validator instead of "looks good to me."

> Built at commercetools (primary author). Honest scope: calibration is "lite" (one run, one scorer, one model family) and the *loop* is still human-checkpointed—explicitly not unattended auto-gating; the gold set is provisional pending review.
